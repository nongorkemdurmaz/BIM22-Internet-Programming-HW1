    ECTS Credit System

    “ECTS is a student-centered credit accumulation and transfer system based on learning outcomes and transparency of the learning process. ECTS aims to facilitate planning, delivery, assessment, recognition and validation for competences and learning components as well as student exchange. Although ECTS is widely used in higher education, it can also be applied in other lifelong learning activities. ECTS credits are based on the workload required for students to achieve expected learning outcomes. Learning outcomes describe what a student will learn, understand and be able to do after successful completion of a learning process. These outputs are associated with the level descriptors in the National and European Qualifications Framework. Workload defines the time students will spend completing all learning activities (such as attending classes, doing projects, self-study, and exams) needed to achieve expected learning outcomes.” In formal education, the defined workload and associated learning outcomes in an academic year correspond to 60 ECTS. Since 1 ECTS credit corresponds to 25 hours at Eskişehir Technical University, the student workload in an academic year is 1500 hours. Eskişehir Technical University uses the ECTS system. "ECTS Credit Test Questionnaire" prepared by experts is applied to all students for each course; ECTS credits for each course are recalculated in line with the results of the survey and the recommendations of the relevant unit and the Quality Commission. It is envisaged that the ECTS credits will be evaluated by applying the survey to all courses every 3 years by the University Administration. In addition, for each course offered in Eskişehir Technical University, a course evaluation questionnaire and a general satisfaction survey are applied to the students, the results are shared with the relevant units, the improvement suggestions of the unit are evaluated in the Quality Commission and the improvement efforts are activated.
